# PRD.md — Product Requirements Document
## Aegis: Your Personal World Intelligence App

**Version:** 1.1 (updated with Knowledge Graph)
**Date:** April 2026
**Status:** Pre-Development

---

## 1. What Is Aegis?

Aegis is a personal AI analyst on your Android phone. It watches 26 open-source intelligence feeds (via the Crucix backend) every 15 minutes, synthesises what is happening in the world, generates curated news articles with context and implications, learns what kinds of events matter to *you specifically*, and lets you have real spoken or typed conversations with an AI that has been watching the world on your behalf.

Over time, Aegis builds a **knowledge graph** — a persistent map of how world events connect to each other. Every article the AI generates adds nodes and edges: "sanctions event → correlated with → energy price spike → preceded → VIX surge". The longer Aegis runs, the richer this graph becomes, and the more contextual and historically grounded its answers get.

Think of it as having a well-read analyst friend who reads everything happening in global finance, geopolitics, conflict, climate, and markets — remembers every connection they've ever noticed — and then talks to you about it in plain human language.

---

## 2. The Problem It Solves

The world produces an overwhelming amount of significant information every hour. Most of it is scattered across RSS feeds, government APIs, financial terminals, OSINT Telegram channels, and academic databases. The average person has no way to cross-correlate a spike in military flight activity over the Black Sea with a sudden move in energy futures and a radiation reading anomaly near a nuclear plant — but these things are often connected.

Aegis removes the noise, connects the signals, builds a memory of those connections over time, and briefs you in a format that respects your time and fits your interests.

---

## 3. Who Is This For?

This is a single-user personal tool, not a consumer product. The target user is one person (you, the developer) who:

- Wants to stay deeply informed on geopolitics, finance, and world events
- Has no time to read 40 sources a day
- Wants to ask "why is this happening?" and get a thoughtful, grounded, historically-aware answer
- Enjoys being briefed like a decision-maker, not a news consumer

> **This is NOT designed to be scaled or launched to the public.** It is your personal intelligence terminal on your phone.

## 4. What Aegis IS

### 4.1 Core Features (Must Have)

**Module-Based Onboarding**
- On first launch, the app asks which domains you care about: Geopolitics, Finance & Markets, Conflict Tracking, Climate & Environment, Tech & Innovation, Health & Epidemics, Trade & Sanctions
- These selections drive which Crucix data sources are emphasised in content generation
- You can change module preferences at any time in settings

**Personal Dashboard**
- A single home screen showing live tiles for your selected modules
- Each tile shows the most recent signal from that domain (e.g. "VIX: 28.4 ↑" or "3 new ACLED events in Sudan")
- A top section titled "Curated For You" showing 3–5 AI-generated news articles based on recent sweep data
- Articles are real-length (400–800 words), not summaries — they include historical context, implications, and cross-domain connections drawn from both the current sweep and the accumulated knowledge graph
- A live news ticker at the bottom (merged headlines from Crucix feeds)

**AI-Generated Articles**
- Each article is generated by Gemini using fresh Crucix sweep data plus relevant graph context retrieved from Neo4j
- Articles explain *what* is happening, *why* it is happening (historical context), and *what it might mean* (implications)
- Articles cross-reference signals from multiple domains (e.g. connecting a geopolitical event to a market move)
- Articles include a "sources" section listing which Crucix feeds were used
- Over time, articles get richer as the knowledge graph accumulates more connections

**Knowledge Graph (Graph RAG backend)**
- Every article generated causes Gemini to also extract up to 5 entity relationships: e.g. `{source: "Russia", target: "European gas prices", relationship: "escalated", confidence: 0.85}`
- These are stored in Neo4j AuraDB as a persistent, growing graph of world event connections
- When generating articles or answering questions, the backend queries this graph (2-hop traversal) and injects the relevant connections as additional context into the Gemini prompt — this is the RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) step
- The graph gets smarter the longer the app runs — early answers use only current data; after weeks, answers draw on accumulated causal chains
- Explore the full graph anytime via Neo4j Browser (desktop web interface, not the Android app)

**Conversation Mode**
- A full chatbot interface (like Gemini or ChatGPT) where you can ask the AI anything about current events
- The AI has access to the latest Crucix sweep data AND relevant knowledge graph context on every message
- Supports typed text input
- Supports voice input (speech-to-text via Android's SpeechRecognizer API)
- Supports voice output (text-to-speech via Android's TextToSpeech API)
- The AI can speak in "briefing mode" — you tap a button and it delivers a spoken briefing of the day, which you can interrupt at any time
- All conversations are stored and accessible in a history view

**Notifications**
- Three priority tiers: 🔴 FLASH (breaking, high urgency), 🟡 PRIORITY (significant but not immediate), 🔵 ROUTINE (interesting but low urgency)
- FLASH: e.g. a nuclear radiation spike, a stock circuit breaker, a major military escalation
- PRIORITY: e.g. a central bank policy change, a new sanctions list, an unusual flight pattern
- ROUTINE: e.g. a new economic indicator release, a Telegram OSINT post, a patent filing
- User can set Do Not Disturb hours, mute specific module notifications, and choose which tiers to receive per-module
- Tapping a notification opens the relevant article or data tile

**Recommendation Algorithm**
- Tracks time spent reading each article, scroll depth, explicit likes/dislikes, and whether the article was shared
- Uses weighted scoring: heavy on time-on-article, medium on explicit likes, light on module category
- Periodically the AI asks "Did you find that useful?" to collect explicit feedback
- Algorithm still introduces 20% "discovery content" from slightly outside your usual pattern
- No external ML platform needed — plain code in `recommendationEngine.js`

**On-Demand Podcast Generation**
- A "Generate Podcast" button on articles and the dashboard
- Generates a two-person AI dialogue (NotebookLM-style) using Gemini for the script and Google Cloud TTS for the voices
- Generated as an MP3, plays inline with a simple audio player
- Podcast script benefits from graph context — the two voices can reference historical connections the graph has surfaced

### 4.2 Secondary Features (Should Have)

- Dark mode (default, terminal aesthetic)
- Pull-to-refresh on dashboard
- Article bookmarking / saving
- Manual "Refresh Now" trigger
- Settings: module toggles, notification preferences, voice settings, TTS speed

### 4.3 Deferred Features (Won't Build in v1)

- Knowledge graph visualisation inside the Android app (graph is explorable via Neo4j Browser on desktop in v1; an in-app visualisation is Phase 13)
- Social features of any kind
- Android home screen widget
- Wear OS support
- Multi-account or multi-user support
- Custom RSS feed addition by the user
- Image generation for articles
- Offline mode beyond cached content
- Graph editing or manual node/edge creation

## 5. What Aegis is NOT

| Not This | Why |
|---|---|
| A social media platform | No user accounts other than yours, no sharing or following |
| A trading terminal | Intelligence awareness only. No buy/sell buttons. No brokerage integration |
| A general-purpose chatbot | The AI is grounded in Crucix data and the knowledge graph. Not a replacement for ChatGPT for unrelated tasks |
| A news aggregator / RSS reader | We generate our own articles from data signals. We do not display raw external articles |
| A multi-user app | Single-user personal tool. No admin panel, no subscription management |
| A real-time streaming dashboard | Refreshes every 15 minutes, not millisecond streams |
| A custom data collector | We do NOT write new data collection code. That is Crucix's job |
| A competitor to Bloomberg Terminal | Intelligence summaries only, no charting or financial analysis tools |
| A map/globe interface | Crucix has one. The mobile app does not replicate it |
| A podcast platform | On-demand generation only. No third-party podcast catalogues |
| A knowledge graph editor | The graph is built automatically by the AI. You do not manually curate it in v1 |

## 6. User Flows

### 6.1 First-Time Setup
```
Install App → Sign In / Create Account (Supabase Auth)
→ Welcome Screen → Module Selection (checkboxes, at least 1 required)
→ Brief explanation of what each module tracks
→ Notification permission prompt (with tier explanation)
→ Dashboard loads with first-run data fetch
```

### 6.2 Daily Usage
```
Open App → Dashboard → Scroll "Curated For You" articles
→ Tap article → Read full article → Like/Dislike → Back
→ Tap conversation button → Voice or text conversation with AI
→ Ask follow-up questions → Close conversation
→ Receive FLASH notification → Tap → Read article about the event
```

### 6.3 Podcast Generation
```
Tap "Generate Podcast" on article OR dashboard
→ Loading screen (15–30 seconds)
→ Inline audio player appears
→ Play two-person dialogue discussing the topic
→ Pause / resume / share audio file
```

### 6.4 Knowledge Graph Exploration (desktop, not Android)
```
Open Neo4j Browser at your AuraDB instance URL
→ Run Cypher query: MATCH (n)-[r]->(m) RETURN n, r, m LIMIT 50
→ Explore interactive graph of accumulated world event connections
→ Filter by relationship type, date range, entity name
```

## 7. Data & Privacy

- All personal data (conversations, preferences, reading history) stored in your private Supabase instance
- Knowledge graph (entities and relationships) stored in your private Neo4j AuraDB instance
- No data shared with any third party except the AI API (Gemini) which receives anonymised content queries
- Article content generated from public open-source data only
- No personal information ever sent to Crucix feeds
- API keys stored in Android Keystore (on-device), never in shared preferences or plain text

## 8. Performance Requirements

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Dashboard initial load | < 2 seconds (cached data) |
| Article open time | < 1 second |
| New article generation | < 10 seconds |
| Voice input latency | < 500ms to begin transcribing |
| Sweep-to-notification delivery | < 2 minutes from sweep completion |
| Podcast generation | < 60 seconds |
| Graph query (2-hop Cypher) | < 500ms |
| Crash rate | < 0.5% of sessions |

---

## 9. Success Criteria (for v1)

- You open the app every day and find at least one article you did not know about
- You can ask the AI a question about a current event and get a grounded, sourced answer
- After 2 weeks, the AI's answers noticeably reference historical connections ("this mirrors what happened in March when...")
- Notifications do not feel noisy or irrelevant
- The recommendation engine noticeably improves over the first two weeks
- Podcast generation works reliably on demand
